Hello & Welcome


My name is Irina, and for the past 14 years I’ve dedicated my work to supporting children, teens, and families—especially those with autism, speech and language delays, and other developmental differences. My passion is helping children unlock their unique potential while giving parents the tools, strategies, and confidence to create nurturing environments at home.

My journey began with a love for children and curiosity about the many ways they communicate with the world. That led me to earn a B.A. in Human Communication Disorders from California State University, Fullerton, and to train as a speech therapist. Along the way, I became a Registered Behavior Technician (RBT) with advanced training in Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA), giving me strong foundations in both evidence-based and holistic approaches.

Over the years, I have worn many hats:

  • Speech and autism specialist in private homes, schools, and therapy centers.
  • Parent coach guiding families in communication strategies, behavior support, and daily routines.
  • Art therapy facilitator, helping children express themselves through creativity.
  • Sound healing provider, using soothing frequencies and instruments to support sensory regulation and relaxation.
  • Child care and developmental support provider, caring for infants through teens with diverse abilities.
  • Bilingual tutor, teaching both English and Russian to children and families.

Each of these roles has strengthened my belief in a whole-child, whole-family approach. Children thrive when therapy is not only evidence-based but also joyful, creative, and respectful of their individuality. Parents thrive when they feel supported, understood, and equipped with practical tools that truly work in everyday life.
